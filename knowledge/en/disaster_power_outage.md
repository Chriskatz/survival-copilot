# Prolonged Power Outage Survival

> ⚠️ Hackathon demo sample content. In an emergency call 911 / 112 / your local emergency number; contact your utility for outage and downed-line reports.

## Carbon monoxide — the silent killer

**Never run a generator, grill, camp stove, or charcoal indoors, in a garage, or near windows** — they produce carbon monoxide (CO) that kills with no smell or warning. Run generators outdoors only, at least 6 m (20 ft) from doors, windows, and vents. If anyone develops headache, dizziness, or nausea, get them to fresh air immediately.

## Food and water safety

Keep the fridge and freezer doors closed: a full freezer holds safe temperature about 48 hours, a fridge about 4 hours. When in doubt, throw it out — discard perishable food held above 4 °C (40 °F) for over 2 hours. If water service or pressure is affected, store water and follow any boil-water notice.

## Staying warm or cool safely

In cold, layer clothing, close off one room, and use blankets; do not use a gas stove or oven for heat (CO risk). In heat, move to the lowest, shadiest floor, drink water often, and use battery or hand fans; check on elderly neighbours, who are most at risk.

## Light, power, and communication

Use battery or crank flashlights, not candles (fire risk). Conserve phone battery: lower screen brightness, switch to low-power mode, and prefer text messages over calls. A car can charge a phone via USB — but only run the engine outdoors, never in a closed garage.

## Medical and vulnerable people

Plan ahead for anyone relying on powered medical equipment (oxygen, refrigerated medicine like insulin). Keep a backup power source and your utility's medical-priority registration if available. If life-sustaining equipment fails and there is no backup, call emergency services.
