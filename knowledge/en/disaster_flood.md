# Flood Survival

> ⚠️ Hackathon demo sample content. In a real flood, follow official evacuation orders and call 911 / 112 / your local emergency number.

## Move to higher ground immediately

At the first warning, move to the highest safe floor or higher ground. Act early — floodwater can rise faster than you can walk. Take a charged phone, water, and essential medicine. Do not wait to "see how bad it gets."

## Never walk or drive through floodwater

**Just 15 cm (6 in) of moving water can knock you off your feet; 30 cm (1 ft) can float a car.** Turn Around, Don't Drown. Floodwater hides downed power lines, open drains, debris, and sewage. If your vehicle is caught in rising water, abandon it and move to higher ground if you can do so safely.

## If trapped indoors by rising water

Go up, not into a closed attic where you could be trapped. Move to the roof only if necessary and signal for help with a bright cloth, light, or whistle. Avoid touching electrical switches or appliances while standing in water.

## Electrical and gas safety

If water is entering the building and it is safe to reach the panel, switch off electricity at the main breaker — **never touch electrical equipment while wet or standing in water**. If you smell gas, shut it off and leave. Do not return until authorities say it is safe.

## After the flood — water and hygiene

Assume floodwater and anything it touched is contaminated. **Do not drink tap water until officials confirm it is safe** — use bottled or boiled/disinfected water. Discard food that contacted floodwater. Wash hands often and keep wounds clean and covered to prevent infection.
