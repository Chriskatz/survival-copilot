# Earthquake Survival

> ⚠️ Hackathon demo sample content. In a real earthquake, follow official alerts and call 911 / 112 / your local emergency number once it is safe to do so.

## During the shaking — Drop, Cover, Hold On

**Drop** to your hands and knees before the quake knocks you down. Take **cover** under a sturdy table or desk; if none is near, crouch against an interior wall away from windows and protect your head and neck with your arms. **Hold on** to your shelter until the shaking stops. Do NOT run outside while the ground is moving — most injuries come from falling objects and broken glass.

## If you are indoors

Stay inside. Do not use elevators. Keep away from windows, mirrors, tall furniture, and anything that can fall. If you are in bed, stay there and cover your head with a pillow unless a heavy fixture is directly above you.

## If you are outdoors or driving

Outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings, power lines, and trees, then drop and cover. If driving, pull over to a clear spot away from overpasses and power lines, stop, and stay inside the vehicle with your seatbelt on until the shaking stops.

## If you are trapped under debris

Do not light a match or lighter (gas leak risk). Cover your mouth and nose with cloth to avoid dust. **Tap on a pipe or wall** or use a whistle so rescuers can locate you — shout only as a last resort, since shouting makes you inhale dangerous dust. Conserve energy and stay as still as possible.

## After the shaking stops

Expect **aftershocks** and Drop-Cover-Hold On again when they come. Check yourself and others for injuries and give first aid. Smell for gas; if you smell it, shut off the supply and leave. Stay out of damaged buildings. Keep phone lines free — use text messages instead of calls.
