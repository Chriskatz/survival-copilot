# Venomous Snake Bite First Aid

> ⚠️ Hackathon demo sample content. For actual snake bites, call 911 / 112 / local emergency immediately and follow rescue personnel instructions.

## What to do immediately

After a venomous snake bite, **stay calm and stop all activity**. Vigorous movement raises heart rate and accelerates venom spread through the bloodstream. Sit or lie down, keep the bite **below heart level** (unless the bite is on the head or neck).

## Things NOT to do (common mistakes)

The old practices of "tourniquet", "cut the wound and suck out venom", "ice packing", "alcohol" are **all wrong or harmful**, and modern first-aid explicitly opposes them. Tight tourniquets cause limb necrosis by cutting blood flow. Incising the wound increases infection risk. Mouth suction poisons the rescuer.

## Pressure immobilisation technique

The correct method is **pressure immobilisation**: wrap an elastic bandage or cloth firmly but **uniformly** around the bite and the entire limb above it (band width about 5 cm, snug enough to fit one finger underneath). This slows lymphatic flow without blocking arterial blood. Splint the whole limb with sticks to minimize movement.

## Remove constricting items

Immediately remove rings, watches, tight sleeves near the bite. **Swelling worsens within minutes** and items become impossible to remove later, potentially cutting off circulation.

## Record snake features

If safe, photograph the snake (do NOT risk a second bite). Otherwise note **color, pattern, head shape (triangular or oval), length**. This information helps medical staff identify the species and choose the correct antivenom. Common venomous snakes in Taiwan: pit viper (龜殼花), bamboo snake (赤尾青竹絲), banded krait (雨傘節), cobra (眼鏡蛇), Russell's viper (鎖鏈蛇), hundred-pacer (百步蛇).

## Evacuation and reporting

After pressure immobilisation, **signal for rescue immediately** with location, bite time, and snake description. While waiting, keep the patient warm, calm, and monitor breathing and consciousness. If breathing fails, begin CPR.

## Antivenom

Antivenom is most effective when given early but **must be administered in a hospital** because it can cause allergic reactions. Never inject any medication in the field. The golden window from bite to hospital is within 4 hours.
